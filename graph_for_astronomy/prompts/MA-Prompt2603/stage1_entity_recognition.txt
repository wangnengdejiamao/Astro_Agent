CORE TASK: ENTITY RECOGNITION AND CANONICALIZATION
 

This extraction is strictly sample-centered.

The primary factual unit in experimental papers is the ExperimentalSample.

This means:

\- MaterialSystem = general material platform

\- ExperimentalSample = specific sample under explicit experimental conditions

\- Scalar values belong to ExperimentalSample attributes, not standalone entities

 

Your goal in Stage 1 is to identify the entity nodes that should exist in the graph, assign each one a canonical name, determine its schema type, and record all valid variants and abbreviation mappings.

 

Do NOT extract triples or attributes in this stage.

 

 

\1. FOLLOW THE PROVIDED SCHEMA STRICTLY

 

Only extract entities whose types are supported by the provided schema.

 

Do NOT invent new schema types outside the provided schema.

 

 

\2. SAMPLE-CENTERED ENTITY RECOGNITION PRINCIPLES

 

 A. MaterialSystem

Create a MaterialSystem entity when the text clearly describes a general material platform or overall material system.

 

Use MaterialSystem for:

\- the overall absorber platform

\- the general composition-support system

\- the umbrella material family from which specific tested samples are derived

 

Examples:

\- "FeCu/GDY absorber"

\- "GDY-supported FeCu alloy"

\- "FeCu alloy on graphdiyne"

 

These should be normalized into one canonical MaterialSystem entity.

 

 B. ExperimentalSample

Create an ExperimentalSample entity when the text refers to a specific tested sample under explicit experimental conditions, especially when structure, EM properties, or EM performance are reported.

 

Create separate ExperimentalSample entities when:

\- explicitly compared processing conditions differ

\- two variables are jointly varied and define distinct tested samples

 

Do NOT merge distinct condition-specific samples into one entity.

 

 C. MaterialComponent / CarbonSupport

Use MaterialComponent for compositional entities such as metals, precursors, additives, or non-support components.

 

Use CarbonSupport for carbon-based support frameworks when the text gives them a support role, such as:

\- GDY

\- graphene

\- carbon nanotube

 

If a carbon material is clearly functioning as the support scaffold, prefer CarbonSupport over MaterialComponent.

 

 D. SynthesisMethod

Use SynthesisMethod for named synthesis routes or clearly defined preparation methods.

 

Examples:

\- microwave-assisted synthesis

\- solvothermal synthesis

\- hydrothermal synthesis

\- annealing-assisted synthesis

 

 E. ProcessingCondition

Use ProcessingCondition only for reusable condition concepts, not for every scalar value.

 

Good examples:

\- reductive annealing

\- inert-atmosphere annealing

\- high-temperature annealing

 

Bad examples:

\- 1000°C

\- 4 h

\- 50 wt%

 

These scalar values should NOT become entities.

 

 F. StructureConcept

Use StructureConcept for reusable scientific concepts involving:

\- phase conclusions

\- alloying conclusions

\- composition-analysis conclusions

\- microstructure conclusions

\- morphology conclusions

 

Good examples:

\- StructureConcept_Fe_Cu_Dominant_Phase

\- StructureConcept_Cu_Peak_Weakening

\- StructureConcept_Enhanced_Alloying

\- StructureConcept_Non_Equiatomic_Composition

\- StructureConcept_Particle_Aggregation

\- StructureConcept_Sintering

 

Bad examples:

\- StructureConcept_395_nm

\- StructureConcept_5.12_GHz

\- StructureConcept_1000C

 

Do NOT create StructureConcept nodes for raw scalar values.

 

 G. CharacterizationMethod

Use CharacterizationMethod for explicit characterization or measurement methods.

 

Always use the short normalized method name as the canonical entity name when appropriate.

 

Examples:

\- XRD

\- ICP

\- Cole_Cole_Analysis

\- Electron_Microscopy

\- RL_Measurement

 

Do NOT prepend "CharacterizationMethod_" to the name.

 

 H. LossMechanism

Use LossMechanism for explicit absorption or dissipation mechanisms.

 

Examples:

\- Dielectric_Loss

\- Magnetic_Loss

\- Interfacial_Polarization

\- Dielectric_Relaxation

 

 I. Limitation

Use Limitation for explicit negative constraints, adverse factors, or performance-limiting concepts.

 

Examples:

\- Poor_Dispersion

\- Impedance_Mismatch

\- Particle_Aggregation

\- Sintering

 

 

 \3. DO NOT CREATE SCALAR-VALUE ENTITIES

 

Do NOT create separate entities for scalar values or value-bearing pseudo-concepts.

 

Never create entities such as:

\- EAB_5.12GHz

\- RL_minus48dB

\- ParticleSize_395nm

\- Temperature_1000C

\- Frequency_13.2GHz

 

Scalar values should be handled in Stage 3 as attributes of ExperimentalSample, not as Stage 1 entities.

 

 

 \4. CHARACTERIZATION-AWARE ENTITY RECOGNITION

 

For each characterization method, identify not only the method itself, but also the main explicitly supported finding WHEN that finding should exist as an entity node.

 

This is especially important for:

\- XRD

\- ICP

\- Cole-Cole analysis

\- Electron microscopy

 

 Specific guidance

 

 XRD

If the text explicitly states:

\- phase dominance

\- peak weakening or disappearance

\- alloying

\- interdiffusion

\- phase evolution

 

then recognize the relevant StructureConcept entity.

 

 ICP or elemental analysis

If the text explicitly states:

\- composition deviation

\- trace-element retention

\- non-equiatomic composition

 

then recognize the relevant StructureConcept entity.

 

 Cole-Cole analysis

If the text explicitly states:

\- dielectric relaxation

\- semicircle appearance with mechanistic meaning

\- related dielectric-loss interpretation

 

then recognize the relevant LossMechanism entity.

 

 Electron microscopy

If the text explicitly states:

\- aggregation

\- dense morphology

\- poor dispersion

\- sintering

\- morphology-based limitations

 

then recognize the relevant StructureConcept or Limitation entity.

 

If the text only gives particle size as a number, do NOT create a particle-size entity.

 

 \5. ANALYSIS PRESERVATION RULE

 

Do not over-compress analytical findings into generic remarks.

 

If the text explicitly presents a reusable scientific interpretation or conclusion, preserve it as:

\- StructureConcept

\- LossMechanism

\- Limitation

 

Only elevate reusable analytical concepts into nodes.

 

Do NOT elevate raw numbers into nodes.

 

 

 \6. CANONICAL NAMING RULES

 

 A. Unified prefixes

Use fully unified prefixes. Do not mix alternative prefixes.

 

 B. MaterialSystem naming

MaterialSystem entities must always use:

\- "System_{composition}_{support}"

 

Example:

\- System_FeCu_GDY

 

Do NOT use:

\- MaterialSystem_{...}

 

 C. ExperimentalSample naming

ExperimentalSample entities must always use:

\- "Sample_{composition}_{support}_{main_variable}"

 

Examples:

\- Sample_FeCu_GDY_T600

\- Sample_FeCu_GDY_t4h

\- Sample_FeCu_GDY_L50wt

\- Sample_FeCu_GDY_T800_t4h

 

Do NOT use:

\- ExperimentalSample_{...}

\- ExperimentalSample_Sample_{...}

 

 D. StructureConcept naming

StructureConcept entities must always use:

\- "StructureConcept_{normalized_finding}"

 

Examples:

\- StructureConcept_Fe_Cu_Dominant_Phase

\- StructureConcept_Cu_Peak_Weakening

\- StructureConcept_Enhanced_Alloying

\- StructureConcept_Non_Equiatomic_Composition

 

Do NOT use bare variants such as:

\- Fe_Cu_Dominant_Phase

\- Cu_Peak_Weakening

\- Non_Equiatomic_Composition

 

 E. CharacterizationMethod / LossMechanism / Limitation naming

Use short normalized names.

 

Examples:

\- XRD

\- ICP

\- Cole_Cole_Analysis

\- Electron_Microscopy

\- Dielectric_Relaxation

\- Impedance_Mismatch

 

 F. Compressed ExperimentalSample naming logic

Do NOT include all conditions in the sample name by default.

 

Identify the main comparison variable of the experimental series:

\- If the section mainly compares annealing temperature, use temperature in the sample name.

\- If the section mainly compares annealing time, use time in the sample name.

\- If the section mainly compares filler loading, use loading in the sample name.

\- If two variables are explicitly varied together and jointly define the comparison, include both.

\- Keep other fixed background conditions out of the sample name.

 

If a condition is constant across the compared series, do NOT encode it into the sample name.

 

 G. Original composition order

Use the original composition order as stated in the text.

Do NOT permute element order.

 

 

 \7. ABBREVIATION AND VARIANT HANDLING

 

When the same entity appears with different names, abbreviations, or formatting variations, you MUST:

\- identify them as the same entity

\- choose ONE canonical name

\- record the other forms in `variants`

\- add valid abbreviation mappings when appropriate

 

 Parenthetical patterns

Always recognize parenthetical patterns as explicit abbreviation definitions.

 

Examples:

\- "X-ray diffraction (XRD)" or "XRD (X-ray diffraction)"

\- "graphdiyne (GDY)" or "GDY (graphdiyne)"

 

Rules:

\- extract both the full form and abbreviation

\- use the canonical name according to the naming policy for that schema type

\- do NOT create separate entities for abbreviation and full form if they are the same entity

 

 Validation rules

Create an abbreviation/variant mapping only if the two forms refer to the SAME entity.

 

These are NOT abbreviation relationships:

\- different chemical substances

\- different compositions

\- different hydration states

\- different concentrations

\- entities mentioned together for comparison

\- different condition-specific samples

 

 Generic referents

If the text uses generic role words such as:

\- the sample

\- this material

\- the absorber

\- the product

\- the alloy

\- the support

 

and they clearly refer to a specific previously named entity in the same chunk, use the specific entity as canonical_name.

 

Do NOT create a separate entity whose canonical_name is only a generic referent if the specific referent is already identifiable.

 

 

 \8. CONSISTENCY RULES

 

\- Use the same canonical name for the same entity throughout the output.

\- Do not create duplicate entities for the same underlying entity.

\- Do not merge different condition-specific samples into one entity.

\- Do not confuse MaterialSystem with ExperimentalSample.

\- Do not use placeholder or vague forms as canonical names if a more specific valid name is available.

 

 

 \9. SOURCE AND EVIDENCE REQUIREMENTS

 

For each entity:

 

\- `source` must be a contiguous, verbatim excerpt from the input text that clearly contains the entity mention.

\- Prefer a full sentence or a clearly interpretable local phrase.

\- Do NOT paraphrase.

 

For `evidence`:

\- explain why this entity should be recognized

\- explain why the chosen canonical name is appropriate

\- explain why the assigned schema type is correct

\- if relevant, explain why a variant/abbreviation/generic referent is mapped to the same entity

\- if relevant, explain why this is a reusable concept node rather than a scalar attribute
