### CORE TASK: RELATION EXTRACTION

#### 1. USE CANONICAL ENTITY NAMES FROM STAGE 1

**CRITICAL**: You MUST use ONLY the canonical names from Stage 1 entities.

- ✅ Use: `"graphene oxide"` (canonical name from Stage 1)
- ❌ Do NOT use: `"GO"` (variant)

---

#### 2. TRIPLE DIRECTION MUST RESPECT GRAMMATICAL LOGIC

- In passive voice (**"X was [verb]ed by Y"**):
  - X = logical object
  - Y = agent/instrument
  - Triple: `[X, relation, Y]`

  ✅ Correct:  
  `["protein expression", "is_regulated_by", "transcription factor"]`  
  ❌ Wrong:  
  `["transcription factor", "is_regulated_by", "protein expression"]`

- For active voice: follow subject–verb–object order.

---

#### 3. SOURCE = CONTIGUOUS, VERBATIM CONTEXT

- The `source` must be a **contiguous excerpt** mentioning both subject and object.
- Provide sufficient context for verification.

---

#### 4. EVIDENCE = EXPLICIT REASONING

In `evidence`, explain:
- The **grammatical structure** (active/passive)
- Why the **triple direction is correct**
- Why **canonical entity names are used**

---

#### 5. MECHANISM CHAINS—DO NOT COLLAPSE (CRITICAL)

When text describes a **causal or mechanistic chain**, extract **each step as a separate triple**.

**Rules**:
- If "X activates Y, and Y inhibits Z" → extract BOTH: `[X, activates, Y]` AND `[Y, inhibits, Z]`
- Never collapse multi-step chains into one direct relation.
