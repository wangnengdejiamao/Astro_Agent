### CORE TASK: ENTITY RECOGNITION AND CANONICALIZATION

#### 1. ENTITY CANONICALIZATION BASED ON SCHEMA TYPE

The final name of every entity must reflect its **semantic role** AND align with its assigned **schema type**:

##### A. Component-Level Entities (`ChemicalCompound`, `Salt`, `Solvent`, `Additive`)
- Extract **only the canonical chemical substance name**, stripping ALL contextual modifiers:
  - ❌ Remove: concentrations ("0.2 M", "5 wt%"), ratios ("3:7"), states ("anhydrous", "hexahydrate" → keep as attribute), procedures ("added", "dissolved"), qualifiers ("excess", "trace").
  - ✅ Keep: standardized chemical name or formula.
- Normalize formulas to ASCII (PubChem-style):
  - `$In(NO_{3})_{3}·6H_{2}O$` → `In(NO3)3·6H2O`
  - `LiPF₆` → `LiPF6`
  - `EC` → retain if standard abbreviation; otherwise use `ethylene carbonate`

- **⚠️ NO GENERIC ROLE WORDS AS ENTITY NAMES (指代/泛指词规避)**  
  When the text uses a **generic role word** (e.g. "the additive", "this compound", "the electrolyte", "the salt") to **refer to** a specific substance already named in the same chunk, you MUST use the **specific substance name** as the canonical entity name, NOT the generic word.
  - ❌ Wrong: canonical_name = `"additive"` when the source says "Herein, In(NO3)3·6H2O is chosen... The additive promotes" — here "the additive" refers to In(NO3)3·6H2O.
  - ✅ Right: one entity with canonical_name = `"In(NO3)3·6H2O"` (or `"In(NO3)3"`), with variants including "additive" only as a referring term in context; do NOT output a separate entity named "additive".
  - Rule: If elsewhere in the chunk a **specific compound** (e.g. In(NO3)3·6H2O, LiPF6, FEC) is introduced by name, and later the text says "the additive", "this compound", "the electrolyte", "the salt", etc., treat that as **the same entity** — canonical_name = the specific substance, and list the generic phrase only as context/variant if needed. **Never** create an entity whose canonical_name is only a generic role word ("additive", "compound", "material", "electrolyte", "solvent") when it clearly refers to a specific substance already named in the chunk.

  Examples:
  - Source: "0.2M In(NO₃)₃·6H₂O was used."  
    → Entity: `"In(NO3)3"` (type: `Salt`)  
  - Source: "EC/DMC (3:7 v/v)"  
    → Entities: `"ethylene carbonate"` (type: `Solvent`), `"dimethyl carbonate"` (type: `Solvent`)
  - Source: "Herein, In(NO3)3·6H2O is chosen as one typical case. The additive promotes the formation of SEI."  
    → Entity: `"In(NO3)3·6H2O"` (type: `Additive` or `Salt`), with variants e.g. ["In(NO3)3", "additive" in context]. Do NOT output a second entity with canonical_name `"additive"`.

##### B. System-Level Entities (`Electrolyte`, `Anode`, `Cathode`)
- Preserve **essential compositional or structural descriptors** that define the system:
  - ✅ Keep: "electrolyte with FEC", "NMC811 cathode", "Si anode coated with carbon"
  - ❌ Remove: performance claims ("showing high stability"), measurement results ("with 100 ppm water")

##### C. `Property` and `PerformanceMetric`
- Use **standardized, value-free names**:
  - ✅ `"water content"`, `"ionic conductivity"`, `"Coulombic efficiency"`
  - ❌ `"conductivity of 8.5 mS/cm"`, `"efficiency >99%"`

##### D. Consistency
- Use the **same canonical name** for an entity across all outputs.

##### E. MECHANISM AND INTERMEDIATE ENTITIES (FOR MULTI-HOP REASONING)

When the text describes **causal or mechanistic chains**, you MUST identify **intermediate entities** that serve as bridges for multi-hop reasoning:

- **Interfacial layers / interfaces**: SEI, CEI, LiF-rich SEI, solid electrolyte interphase, etc. — use `InterfacialLayer` if in schema, else `ChemicalCompound` or `Property`
- **Failure modes / degradation phenomena**: lithium dendrite formation, electrolyte decomposition, TM dissolution, dendrite growth, etc. — use `FailureMode` or `SideReaction` if in schema, else `Property`
- **Mechanism descriptions**: "suppression of lithium dendrite growth", "formation of stable SEI", etc. — use `StabilizationMechanism` or `Phenomenon` if in schema, else `Property`

**Rules**:
- ✅ Extract "lithium dendrite formation" (or "lithium dendrite growth") when text says "inhibiting the formation of lithium dendrites" or "suppresses lithium dendrite growth"
- ✅ Extract "electrolyte decomposition" when text says "inhibits the decomposition of electrolyte" or "suppresses electrolyte decomposition"
- ✅ Extract SEI, CEI as first-class entities (not just abbreviations) — they are critical bridge entities
- ✅ Use schema types from the provided schema; if InterfacialLayer/FailureMode/SideReaction are not in schema, use the closest available type (e.g., Property, ChemicalCompound)
- ❌ Do NOT skip these entities — they enable multi-hop reasoning from additives/components to performance outcomes

#### 2. ABBREVIATION AND SYNONYM HANDLING (CRITICAL)

**⚠️ CRITICAL: Abbreviation relationships MUST represent TRUE synonyms/abbreviations of the SAME entity, NOT different entities that happen to appear together in the text.**

When the same entity appears with **different names, abbreviations, or formatting variations**, you MUST:

1. **Identify all variants** of the same entity:
   - **Abbreviations**: `"EC"` ↔ `"ethylene carbonate"`, `"DMC"` ↔ `"dimethyl carbonate"`, `"FEC"` ↔ `"fluoroethylene carbonate"`
   - **Synonyms**: `"LiPF6"` ↔ `"lithium hexafluorophosphate"`, `"NMC811"` ↔ `"LiNi0.8Mn0.1Co0.1O2"`
   - **Case variations**: `"LiPF6"` ↔ `"lipf6"` ↔ `"LiPF₆"` (normalize to standard case/formula)
   - **Formatting differences**: `"Cu(NO3)2"` ↔ `"Cu(NO₃)₂"` ↔ `"copper nitrate"` (normalize to ASCII formula)
   - **Parenthetical explanations**: `"XRD"` ↔ `"X-ray diffraction"`, `"TEM"` ↔ `"transmission electron microscopy"`, `"NMR"` ↔ `"nuclear magnetic resonance"`

2. **CRITICAL: Parenthetical Abbreviation Patterns** (MUST EXTRACT):
   
   **Pattern 1: Abbreviation (Full Name)**
   - Format: `"ABBREV (Full Name)"` or `"ABBREV (full name)"`
   - Example: `"XRD (X-ray diffraction)"`, `"TEM (transmission electron microscopy)"`, `"EC (ethylene carbonate)"`
   - **Action**: Extract both as the SAME entity, use full name as canonical
   
   **Pattern 2: Full Name (Abbreviation)**
   - Format: `"Full Name (ABBREV)"` or `"full name (ABBREV)"`
   - Example: `"X-ray diffraction (XRD)"`, `"ethylene carbonate (EC)"`
   - **Action**: Extract both as the SAME entity, use full name as canonical
   
   **Key Rules for Parenthetical Patterns**:
   - ✅ **ALWAYS** recognize that text in parentheses immediately following or preceding a term is likely an explanation/definition
   - ✅ **ALWAYS** extract both the abbreviation and the full name when they appear together
   - ✅ **ALWAYS** use the full name as the canonical entity name (prefer full form)
   - ❌ **NEVER** ignore parenthetical explanations — they are explicit definitions
   - ❌ **NEVER** create separate entities for the abbreviation and full name

3. **⚠️ STRICT VALIDATION: What is NOT an abbreviation relationship**:
   
   **CRITICAL RULES - These are NEVER abbreviation relationships**:
   
   - ❌ **DIFFERENT CHEMICAL SUBSTANCES**: Two different chemical compounds are NEVER synonyms
   - ❌ **MENTIONED TOGETHER**: Just because two entities appear in the same sentence does NOT make them synonyms
   - ❌ **COMPARISON/CONTRAST**: Entities mentioned for comparison are NOT synonyms
   - ❌ **DIFFERENT HYDRATION STATES**: Different hydration states are NOT abbreviations, they are attributes
   - ❌ **DIFFERENT CONCENTRATIONS**: Same compound with different concentrations are NOT synonyms
   
   **VALIDATION CHECKLIST** (MUST pass ALL to create abbreviation relationship):
   - ✅ Are they referring to the EXACT SAME chemical substance/entity?
   - ✅ Is one an abbreviation/short form of the other?
   - ✅ Is one a formatting variant of the other?
   - ✅ Is there explicit parenthetical definition?
   - ❌ Are they different substances mentioned together? → NO abbreviation relationship
   - ❌ Are they being compared or contrasted? → NO abbreviation relationship

4. **Choose ONE canonical name** as the primary entity name:
   - Prefer: **standardized chemical formula** (e.g., `"LiPF6"`, `"Cu(NO3)2"`) or **IUPAC/common name** (e.g., `"ethylene carbonate"`)
   - Use the **most complete/full form** when multiple variants appear
   - **For parenthetical patterns**: Always use the **full name** as canonical (e.g., `"X-ray diffraction"` not `"XRD"`)

5. **Critical rules**:
   - ✅ **ALWAYS** recognize parenthetical patterns as explicit abbreviation definitions
   - ✅ **ALWAYS** use the same canonical name for an entity
   - ✅ **ALWAYS** validate that two terms refer to the SAME entity before creating abbreviation relationship
   - ✅ **ALWAYS** resolve referents: if "the additive" / "this compound" in the chunk refers to a specific substance already named (e.g. In(NO3)3·6H2O), use that substance as the canonical entity; do not use "additive" or "compound" as canonical_name
   - ❌ **NEVER** create separate entities for abbreviations/variants — they must share the same canonical name
   - ❌ **NEVER** create abbreviation relationships between DIFFERENT chemical substances
   - ❌ **NEVER** create abbreviation relationships just because two entities appear in the same sentence
   - ❌ **NEVER** use a generic role word ("additive", "compound", "electrolyte", "material", "solvent" as a bare referent) as canonical_name when the chunk has already named the specific substance that the referent points to
