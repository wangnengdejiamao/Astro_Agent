### Dimension 1 – Triple Accuracy

Judge whether the triple is **correctly supported** by the text:

1. Does the `source` fragment, together with nearby context in `chunk_text`, clearly mention the start node, relation, and end node?
2. If the relation is subtle (e.g. "improves", "inhibits", "stabilizes"), is there a clear causal or at least strongly correlated statement in the text?
3. Is the `evidence` explanation logically consistent with the text, without hallucinating facts that are not present or reasonably implied?
4. If the triple involves interpretation (e.g. "additive improves cycling stability"), is that interpretation clearly justified by performance data, comparisons, or explicit author claims?

Think of **accuracy_score** as:

- Close to **1.0** when the triple is explicitly and unambiguously supported (e.g. text states that adding `In(NO3)3·6H2O` increases capacity retention and CE, and the triple is "In(NO3)3·6H2O improves cycling stability of Li‖NCM811 cell").  
- Around **0.6–0.8** when the triple is partially supported but requires some inference, or the wording is slightly vague.  
- Below **0.4** when the relation is weakly or not supported (e.g. the text only mentions that a method "was used" but the triple claims it "improves performance").

### Dimension 2 – Triple Usefulness / Importance

Now judge whether this triple is **useful and important** within the scientific context of the local paragraph (where `source` lives):

1. Does the triple capture a **core claim or key result** of the paragraph?  
   - Example of *useful*: "In(NO3)3·6H2O additive improves capacity retention of Li‖NCM811 cells from 57.7% to 90.0% after 300 cycles."  
   - Example of *less useful but accurate*: "Lithium metal batteries have high theoretical specific capacity" in a background/introduction sentence.
2. Would this triple be helpful for downstream tasks such as:
   - Understanding why a certain additive or mechanism works,
   - Explaining performance improvements or degradation mechanisms,
   - Summarizing the main contributions of the paper?
3. Is the information **specific and non‑trivial** (e.g. involving quantitative improvements, mechanisms, or causal relationships), rather than generic background knowledge?

Think of **usefulness_score** as:

- Close to **1.0** for triples that encode key insights, mechanisms, or main performance results in the paragraph.  
- Around **0.5–0.7** for supporting but secondary details.  
- Below **0.4** for triples that are peripheral, generic background, or not particularly informative for understanding the main scientific message.
