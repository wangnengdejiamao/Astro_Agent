### CORE TASK: ENTITY RECOGNITION AND CANONICALIZATION

#### 1. ENTITY CANONICALIZATION BASED ON SCHEMA TYPE

The final name of every entity must reflect its **semantic role** AND align with its assigned **schema type**:

##### A. Substance/Component Entities (`ChemicalCompound`, `Material`, `Reagent`, `Substance`)
- Extract **only the canonical substance name**, stripping contextual modifiers:
  - ❌ Remove: concentrations ("0.2 M", "5 wt%"), ratios ("3:7"), states ("anhydrous"), procedures ("added", "dissolved")
  - ✅ Keep: standardized chemical name, formula, or common name
- Normalize formulas to ASCII:
  - `$Fe_2O_3$` → `Fe2O3`
  - `CO₂` → `CO2`

- **⚠️ NO GENERIC ROLE WORDS AS ENTITY NAMES**
  When text uses generic role words ("the additive", "this compound", "the catalyst", "the reagent") to refer to a specific substance already named, use the **specific substance name** as canonical, NOT the generic word.

##### B. System/Composite Entities (`System`, `Mixture`, `Composite`)
- Preserve essential compositional descriptors:
  - ✅ Keep: "composite with graphene", "alloy containing Ni", "solution of NaCl"
  - ❌ Remove: performance claims, measurement results

##### C. `Property` and `Measurement`
- Use standardized, value-free names:
  - ✅ "melting point", "conductivity", "efficiency", "expression level"
  - ❌ "conductivity of 8.5 mS/cm", "efficiency >99%"

##### D. `Process` and `Phenomenon`
- Extract processes and phenomena as entities when they are the subject of study:
  - ✅ "crystallization", "degradation", "proliferation", "oxidation"

##### E. Consistency
- Use the **same canonical name** for an entity across all outputs.

#### 2. ABBREVIATION AND SYNONYM HANDLING (CRITICAL)

**⚠️ CRITICAL: Abbreviation relationships MUST represent TRUE synonyms/abbreviations of the SAME entity.**

When the same entity appears with **different names, abbreviations, or formatting variations**:

1. **Identify all variants**:
   - **Abbreviations**: "GO" ↔ "graphene oxide", "ROS" ↔ "reactive oxygen species"
   - **Synonyms**: "sodium chloride" ↔ "NaCl", "tumor necrosis factor" ↔ "TNF"
   - **Case variations**: "p53" ↔ "P53"
   - **Formatting differences**: normalize to ASCII

2. **Parenthetical Abbreviation Patterns** (MUST EXTRACT):
   - "GO (graphene oxide)" → Extract both as SAME entity
   - "graphene oxide (GO)" → Extract both as SAME entity
   - Use **full name** as canonical when available

3. **What is NOT an abbreviation**:
   - ❌ Different substances mentioned together
   - ❌ Comparison/contrast entities
   - ❌ Different concentrations/states of same substance (these are attributes)

4. **Choose ONE canonical name**:
   - Prefer: **standardized name** (IUPAC, common scientific name)
   - Use the **most complete/full form** when multiple variants appear
