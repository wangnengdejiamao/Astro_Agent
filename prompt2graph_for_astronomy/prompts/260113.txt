##### 2.3 Chemical Formula Normalization (MANDATORY)

When a chemical entity is expressed as a chemical formula, you MUST normalize the formula into a PubChem-compatible ASCII format.

This includes, but is not limited to:

- LaTeX-style subscripts and superscripts:
  - `$In(NO_{3})_{3}$` → `In(NO3)3`
  - `$CuSO_{4}·5H_{2}O$` → `CuSO4·5H2O`

- Unicode subscripts or superscripts:
  - `In(NO₃)₃` → `In(NO3)3`
  - `PF₆⁻` → `PF6-`
  - `SO₄²⁻` → `SO4^2-` or `SO4-2`

- Mathematical or typographical symbols should be converted to their plain-text equivalents where possible.

IMPORTANT:
- This normalization applies ONLY to chemical formulas.
- Do NOT alter non-chemical text, measurement units, or narrative descriptions.
- Do NOT invent or guess chemical formulas not explicitly present in the source.
- The normalized formula MUST represent the same chemical substance as the original expression.

Use the normalized ASCII formula consistently:
- in `triples`
- in `attributes`
- in `entity_types`

#### 4. SOURCE = CONTIGUOUS, VERBATIM CONTEXT
- The `source` must be a **contiguous excerpt** from the input text (1–2 sentences or even the entire paragraph) that **explicitly mentions both the subject and object**.
- It should provide **sufficient context** for a domain expert to verify the relationship without external knowledge.
- Do NOT truncate or paraphrase.

#### 5. TRIPLE DIRECTION MUST MATCH GRAMMATICAL LOGIC — ESPECIALLY IN PASSIVE VOICE
- In passive constructions like **"X was [verb]ed by Y"**, **"X is [verb]ed using Y"**, or **"X was determined via Y"**:
  - **X is the OBJECT (patient/theme) of the action**
  - **Y is the AGENT/INSTRUMENT (subject/tool) performing the action**
  - The triple must be: `[X, "relation", Y]`
  
  Examples:
  - Source: `"The water content was measured by Karl Fischer titration."`  
    ✅ Correct triple: `["water content", "is_measured_by", "Karl Fischer titration"]`  
    ❌ Wrong triple: `["Karl Fischer titration", "is_measured_by", "water content"]`

  - Source: `"Cell viability was assessed using MTT assay."`  
    ✅ Correct: `["cell viability", "is_assessed_using", "MTT assay"]`

  - Source: `"The structure was solved via X-ray crystallography."`  
    ✅ Correct: `["structure", "is_solved_via", "X-ray crystallography"]`

- **Never reverse the direction** just because Y appears after "by". The grammatical subject of a passive sentence is the *logical object* of the action.

---

#### 6. RELATION SEMANTICS
- Use precise relations.
- Prefer:
  - "is_associated_with"
  - "corresponds_to"
  - "results_in"
  - "exhibits"
  - "is_measured_by"
  - "is_compared_with"
- Avoid causal overclaiming unless explicitly stated.

---

#### 7. TRIPLE SEMANTICS MUST MATCH SOURCE
- Reconstructing the triple as “[subject] [relation] [object]” should **not distort, overclaim, or simplify** the original claim.
- Choose relation labels that reflect **linguistic nuance**:  
  - Use “associated_with”, “correlates_with”, or “linked_to” for observational/statistical claims.  
  - Use “causes”, “enhances”, “inhibits” only if the source uses **causal or mechanistic language**.

---

#### 9. EVIDENCE = GRAMMATICAL + LOGICAL JUSTIFICATION
In `evidence`, you **MUST**:
- Identify whether the sentence is **active or passive voice**;
- For passive voice, explicitly state:  
  > “This is a passive construction: ‘[full clause]’. The entity ‘X’ is the logical object being [verb]ed, and ‘Y’ is the method/agent introduced by ‘by/using/via’. Therefore, the triple direction is X → relation → Y.”
- Explain why reversing would misrepresent causality/measurement flow

Example:
> Source: `"The hydrogel loaded with doxorubicin released 80% of the drug within 24 hours."`  
> Evidence: `"The grammatical subject is 'The hydrogel loaded with doxorubicin' — a composite drug delivery system. The verb 'released' applies to the entire hydrogel, not doxorubicin alone. Extracting 'doxorubicin' as subject would falsely imply the drug self-releases."`

Reversing the order would imply the titration is being measured by the concentration, which is illogical."
